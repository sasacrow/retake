Haarlem’s Sights and Attractions

You can easily discover Haarlem’s sights and attractions on a leisurely stroll through the city.

Haarlem

Haarlem, capital city of the province Noord-Holland. It is a medium sized city, with around 160.000 inhabitants and is known to have a good atmosphere. If you want to go shopping, Haarlem is your place to be, with something for every target group. In 1994, 2004 and 2005 it was proclaimed as the best shopping city of The Netherlands.

Windmill De Adriaan

One of Haarlem’s most distinctive sights is the De Adriaan Windmill. Located on the banks of the river Spaarne, it is a quintessentially Dutch landmark, and a guided tour should not be missed on your trip to Haarlem. From the top of the mill, you have a fantastic view of the historic city centre, along the banks of the river and beyond.

De Jopenkerk

De Jopenkerk (Jopen Church) is a renovated former church which now houses a grand café, restaurant and microbrewery. Head to the charming venue for delicious food and drinks, and naturally for a taste of the famous Jopen beer! De Jopenkerk’s parsonage is a unique location for meetings, while the restaurant itself is also available for hire.

Oute Stad

Lively Oude Stad is Haarlem’s commercial center, its narrow cobbled streets lined with mainstream fashion chains and indie craft stores. Fine-dining restaurants, global eateries, and cheerful cafes cluster on and around the Grote Markt, alongside bars and hip brewpubs. The Frans Hals Museum, in a 17th-century building, shows work by Hals and other Dutch Old Masters, and organ recitals take place at St. Bavo Church.


Shopping

Haarlem has frequently been voted “Best Shopping City” in the Netherlands, and its easy to see why! Haarlem is a popular city for shopping in North Holland. In fact, people come from all over the Netherlands – including Amsterdam – just to shop in Haarlem. The city has deservedly been voted Best Shopping City in the Netherlands many times. Haarlem boasts a diverse range of shops, from fantastic small, quirky boutiques to concept stores to the usual Dutch department stores (HEMA) and chains (like H&M, Zara and Esprit). Haarlem’s compact centre makes it perfect for window shopping and browsing.


The St. Bavo Church in Haarlem

The Grote Kerk or St.-Bavokerk is a Reformed Protestant church and former Catholic cathedral located on the central market square in the Dutch city of Haarlem. Another Haarlem church called the Cathedral of Saint Bavo now serves as the main cathedral for the Roman Catholic Diocese of Haarlem-Amsterdam.

HWA-RANG DRAGON

Hwa-Rang Dragon is a martial art school in the Schalkwijk from Haarlem. It specialises in Taekwondo, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and Mudo. The school offers lessons for both adults and children in all of sports stated. But what exactly are these sports and what's the difference between all of them? Here's an explanation: Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a martial art and combat sport system that focuses on grappling with particular emphasis on ground fighting. Brazilian jiu-jitsu was developed from Kodokan judo ground fighting (newaza) fundamentals that were taught by a number of Japanese individuals including Takeo Yano, Mitsuyo Maeda, Soshihiro Satake, and Isao Okano. Brazilian jiu-jitsu eventually came to be its own defined combat sport through the innovations, practices, and adaptation of judo.
